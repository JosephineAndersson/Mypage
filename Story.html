I've always been a sucker for gaming!

I was four and the computer took about five minutes to even start. In the evenings I would sit in my father's lap and learn how to play computer games. It was difficult but it amazed me.

In the early 2000 I was in middle school and my teacher asked the class “Do you have any computer at home?”. I answered “Yes of course I do” while thinking the answer to the question was obvious (like duh, boomer?) even though many of my classmates said they didn't have any.

As I got home that day I counted how many computers we actually had and I couldn't fit the counting on both of my hands. One on the kitchen table, one in parts in a random corner, one on my room, another one at my brothers. In the office there were multiple.

I was twelve and I got to go with my father to a LAN. I brought my own (enormous and heavy) computer. It was only boys and men there, but I couldn't have cared less. We were all crowded closely together in an old cabin with network cables everywhere. In the middle of the room there was a large board with tournament results. I got to face one of my dad’s friends in Starcraft. I won. My dad was so proud.
It started early. Today I am 28 and my biggest hobby is still computer games and I am happy the apple doesn't fall far from the tree. Combined with my love for gaming is now a passion for digital communities and for raising the ethos of professional players and gamers over all.

I am aiming to work with what I love. Because if there is something life has taught me it’s to follow my dreams and aim for what my heart tells me to.
